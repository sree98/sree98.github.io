001 Apple

NameDrop

End-to-end ownership of NameDrop, iOS's peer-to-peer contact sharing feature. The core challenge: design a UI that communicates secure hardware proximity over CoreBluetooth in under a second of physical contact, for millions of users across every network condition and device state.

Every animation, state transition, and haptic beat had to map to an underlying radio handshake. Invisible infrastructure made tangible. Credited as inventor on US20240419308A1 and US20250247901A1.

SwiftUI CoreBluetooth Peer-to-Peer Networking Haptics

Constraints & Signals

Hardware Constraint

Perceptual Design

Haptic and visual feedback designed to communicate trust and security without words. A zero-literacy UX operating at the speed of reflex.

002 Apple

AirPods Setup & Engraving

Designed and built the setup and engraving flow for AirPods, integrating CloudKit, live device firmware data, and real-time 3D hardware renders via SceneKit and AVFoundation. The UI needed to feel instant while orchestrating asynchronous calls to cloud, firmware, and GPU pipelines simultaneously.

The rendering challenge: personalized hardware previews had to update in real time without dropping a frame. UI responsiveness was non-negotiable even as the render pipeline was in motion.

005 Drexel Research

Lilypad: Special Education Platform

As an undergraduate research assistant at Drexel's College of Computing & Informatics, I helped design the UI for Lilypad, an iPad-based behavioral intervention system for special education classrooms. The interface had to serve teachers, aides, and behavioral specialists working in high-distraction environments while maintaining data accuracy required for federally mandated IEP reports.

The core challenge: reduce complex behavioral data recording to simple, tap-based interactions that could be performed mid-session without breaking flow.

iPad UI Design Accessibility Education Technology Behavioral Health

Constraints & Signals

Attention Constraint

Teachers and behavioral specialists needed to record precise data while actively working with students. UI had to be actionable without sustained focus or breaking classroom engagement.

Compliance & Accuracy

Data collected would become legal documentation for IEPs affecting 5M+ students nationwide. No room for misinterpretation or recording errors.
